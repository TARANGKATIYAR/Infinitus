Gen-AI

Generative AI refers to a class of artificial intelligence systems designed to create new content ‚Äî from text, images, and audio, to video and code ‚Äî by learning patterns and structures from vast data.

Think of it as a creative co-pilot or imagination engine: it doesn't just predict what comes next, it synthesizes new ideas, art, and information that did not exist before.

Unlike traditional AI, which focuses mainly on classification or prediction, generative AI empowers humans to explore limitless creativity and innovation.

Evolution Over the Years

2014

GANs Introduced

Generative Adversarial Networks enabled machines to create realistic images and data by pitting two neural networks against each other.

2017

Transformer Architecture

"Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al.), Introduced the transformer, allowing models to scale massively and understand long-range dependencies in data. Became the architecture behind all major LLMs

2020

Large Language Models

Proved that large-scale unsupervised training could lead to coherent, context-aware text generation. Brought gen-AI to the mainstream: content writing, coding, chatbots, assistants.

2021-22

Diffusion Models for Image-Gen

Replaced GANs for many tasks with more stable, controllable models that generate high-fidelity, creative images from text. AI-powered art tools changed the creative industry.

2023

Multimodal Generative AI

AI became capable of understanding and creating across multiple formats, not just text or images. Applications in document analysis, vision-language tasks, virtual assistants, and education.

2025

Agentic & Tool-Using AI

Models evolved from just generators to autonomous agents ‚Äî capable of doing tasks like web browsing, file editing, coding, and decision-making.
